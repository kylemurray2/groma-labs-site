TerraCheck Marketing Plan & Growth Strategy

Mission: Democratize geophysical safety data for the residential market.
Primary Goal: Establish "Ground Stability" as a standard checklist item in real estate transactions, alongside Radon and Lead Paint.

1. Executive Summary: The "Ink Blot" Strategy

Instead of a "spray and pray" national launch, TerraCheck will deploy an "Ink Blot" strategy. We will saturate a single high-risk geologic zone (Leyden/Arvada, CO) to prove utility and "ground truth" the science. Once the model is profitable and calibrated, we will expand to geologically similar "clones" (e.g., Texas Hill Country).

Why This Works:

Technical Leverage (OPERA): Using NASA OPERA displacement products means we have near-instant scalability. We don't need to process raw radar data, allowing us to focus 100% of our resources on marketing and verification.

Marketing Efficiency: Neighbors trust neighbors. "I live in Leyden" is a stronger pitch than "Global AI Startup."

Liability Sandbox: We validate our "Red Light/Green Light" logic on local soil we understand before automating it for unfamiliar geologies.

2. Target Audience Segments

Primary: The "Anxious Homeowner" (B2C)

Profile: Recently bought in a new development (Candelas/Leyden Rock) or lives in an older home with visible settling (Arvada).

Trigger: "Why is my basement floor heaving?" or "Is it safe to buy here?"

Value Prop: Peace of mind for $29. Cheaper than a structural engineer ($800+).

Secondary: The "Liability-Averse Agent" (B2B)

Profile: Top 10% of agents in Jefferson County (20+ deals/year).

Trigger: Fear of post-closing lawsuits regarding foundation issues.

Value Prop: "The Radon Test for Foundations." A $29 insurance policy to show they did their due diligence.

Tertiary: The Home Inspector (Channel Partner)

Profile: Independent inspectors looking to differentiate from franchises.

Trigger: Wants to offer "Premium" packages.

Value Prop: An effortless upsell ($50 add-on) that requires no extra labor.

3. Phased Growth Roadmap

Phase 1: The "Leyden Lab" (Months 0-3)

Goal: 50 Paid Reports | 5 Testimonials | 1 Agent Partner
Geography: Leyden, Candelas, West Arvada (80007, 80005).

Tactics:

The "Neighbor Scientist" Campaign (Nextdoor/FB Groups):

Action: Post OPERA-derived velocity maps of the neighborhood (anonymized).

Script: "I’m a geophysicist living in Leyden. I downloaded the latest NASA satellite data for our neighborhood to check my foundation. Who wants to see their house?"

Conversion: DM conversation $\to$ Free "Lite" Report $\to$ $29 Upsell for Full PDF.

The Direct Mail "Sniper" Drop:

Action: Send 100 postcards to homes directly on the Leyden Mine boundaries.

Visual: Heatmap of their street. "Your home sits on the mine boundary. Check your stability."

Manual Calibration: Use these first users to verify if satellite "hotspots" match their actual drywall cracks. Crucial for liability testing.

Phase 2: The Agent "Trojan Horse" (Months 4-12)

Goal: 200 Paid Reports | 10 Recurring Agent Users
Geography: Jefferson County (Golden, Lakewood, Wheat Ridge).

Tactics:

The "Safety Briefing" Lunch:

Action: Offer to present a 15-minute "Geologic Risks in Arvada" talk at local brokerage offices (Remax, Keller Williams).

Hook: Bring pizza. Show the map. Scaring them with local landslide data makes you the authority.

Instagram/Meta Targeted Ads:

Audience: Job Title: "Real Estate Agent" + Location: "Jefferson County."

Budget: $200/month.

Creative: "Don't let a shifting foundation kill your deal. Get the TerraCheck report."

Inspector Partnerships:

Action: Email top-rated inspectors. Offer them 5 free report credits to test on their own homes.

Phase 3: The "Clone Markets" (Year 2+)

Goal: 1,000+ Reports/Year | Automated API Pipeline
Geography: Texas Hill Country (Austin/San Antonio), Front Range Corridor.

Tactics:

Rapid Expansion (Leveraging OPERA):

Strategy: Since data processing is solved, we can launch in a new city in 48 hours. We simply download the relevant OPERA frame and ingest it.

Action: Launch "City-Specific" landing pages (e.g., terracheck.com/austin) targeted at known clay-soil neighborhoods.

SEO "Symptom" Content:

Strategy: Capture search traffic for "Drywall cracks Austin" or "Foundation repair cost San Antonio."

Content: Blog posts explaining why (expansive clay) and offering the Report as the first step before calling a repair company.
